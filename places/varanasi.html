Overview

Varanasi, also known as Benares or Kashi, is the spiritual capital of India. It is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and the holiest of the seven sacred cities in Hinduism and Jainism. The city's beating heart is the sacred River Ganges, along whose banks are dozens of ghats—stone steps leading down to the water, where pilgrims perform rituals. Varanasi is a sensory overload of sights, sounds, and smells, offering an unparalleled glimpse into India's profound spirituality and ancient traditions.

Things To Do

  • Take a serene sunrise boat ride on the River Ganges.
  • Witness the spectacular evening Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat.
  • Explore the narrow, winding alleys (galis) of the old city.
  • Visit the sacred Kashi Vishwanath Temple (Golden Temple).
  • Take a short trip to Sarnath, where Buddha gave his first sermon.
  • Shop for world-famous Banarasi silk sarees.

Location

Situated on the banks of the Ganges in Uttar Pradesh, roughly 320 kilometers southeast of the state capital, Lucknow.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Varanasi is from October to March when the weather is cool and pleasant for exploring the ghats.

Top Attractions

Dashashwamedh Ghat Kashi Vishwanath Temple Manikarnika Ghat Sarnath Stupa Ramnagar Fort

How To Reach

By Air: Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport (VNS) connects Varanasi to major Indian cities.

By Train: Varanasi Junction (BSB) is a major railway hub with excellent connectivity nationwide.

By Road: Well-connected by national highways. Overnight buses run from Delhi, Lucknow, and regional hubs.

Travel Tips

- Be respectful at cremation ghats like Manikarnika; avoid taking photos.
- Expect crowds and narrow bustling streets—walk or use cycle rickshaws.
- Try local street food like chaat and lassi, but prefer bottled water.
- Arrive early to get the best spot for the evening Aarti.
