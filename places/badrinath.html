Overview

Badrinath, situated on the banks of the Alaknanda River at an altitude of 3,133 meters, is the most sacred of the Char Dham sites. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, the temple is characterized by its vibrant colors and stunning Dravidian architecture. It is located between the Nar and Narayan mountain ranges, with the majestic Neelkanth peak providing a dramatic backdrop. Badrinath is not only a pilgrimage site but also a place of great natural beauty, offering access to high-altitude hotspots like the Valley of Flowers and Hemkund Sahib.

Things To Do

  • Visit the Badrinath Temple and offer prayers to Lord Badri Vishal.
  • Take a dip in the Tapt Kund, a natural hot sulfur water spring.
  • Visit the Mana Village, known as the 'Last Indian Village' before the Tibet border.
  • Explore the Vyas Gufa and Ganesh Gufa where the Mahabharata was written.
  • Witness the Vasudhara Falls, a spectacular waterfall located 9 km from the temple.
  • Explore the Bheem Pul, a natural stone bridge over the Saraswati River.

Location

Badrinath is located in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, in the Garhwal Himalayas.

Best Time to Visit

The temple is open from May to October/November. May-June and September-October are the most pleasant months for visiting.

Top Attractions

How To Reach

By Air: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun (317 km). Helicopter services are available.

By Train: Rishikesh is the nearest railway station (297 km).

By Road: Well-connected by Road to Rishikesh, Haridwar, and Dehradun.

Travel Tips

- Carry sufficient warm clothing as the weather remains cold throughout the year.
- Carry essential medicines; high-altitude sickness can occur for some travelers.
- Respect the local customs and rituals during your visit to the temple.
- Be prepared for long road journeys due to the mountainous terrain.
