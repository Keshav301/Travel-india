Overview

Haflong, the only hill station in Assam, is a picturesque town often referred to as the "Switzerland of the East." Known for its rolling hills, emerald lakes, and diverse tribal heritage, it is the headquarters of the Dima Hasao district. The town is famous for the Haflong Lake, situated right at its center, surrounded by mist-covered peaks. Its cool climate, lush orchards of pineapples and oranges, and the colorful cultures of its resident tribes like the Dimasas and Zemes make Haflong a unique and refreshing destination in the Northeast.

Things To Do

  • Spend a peaceful evening boating and walking around the beautiful Haflong Lake.
  • Take a short trip to Jatinga (9 km away), famous for the "bird suicide" phenomenon during late monsoon.
  • Go trekking in the Borail Range for panoramic views and a chance to experience the wild flora and fauna.
  • Visit the Maibang ruins to see the historic stone dwellings of the 16th-century Dimasa kingdom.
  • Explore the local market to buy organic honey, handmade tribal textiles, and fresh hill produce.
  • Interact with the local tribes to learn about their unique customs, dances, and weaving traditions.

Location

Haflong is the headquarters of Dima Hasao district, located in the south-central part of Assam.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to April. The monsoons are beautiful but can cause road blocks.

How To Reach

By Air: Silchar Airport (approx. 110 km) or Guwahati Airport (approx. 330 km).

By Train: New Haflong Railway Station is well-connected to Guwahati, Silchar, and Agartala.

By Road: Connected by NH-27. Regular buses and shared taxis ply from Guwahati and Silchar.

Travel Tips

- Carry light woolens even in summer, as evenings can get chilly.
- Be prepared for occasional power outages and limited internet in some remote parts.
- Try the local Dimasa traditional food, strictly using fresh, organic ingredients.
- Hire a local guide if you plan to trek deep into the Borail forests.
