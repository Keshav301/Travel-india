Overview

Ajmer is a historic city in Rajasthan, surrounded by the Aravalli Mountains. It is most famous for the Ajmer Sharif Dargah, the shrine of the Sufi saint Moinuddin Chishti. This holy site attracts millions of pilgrims of all faiths from across the world, making Ajmer a symbol of communal harmony. Beyond the Dargah, the city boasts the beautiful Ana Sagar Lake, several historical monuments like the Taragarh Fort, and the unique Adhai Din Ka Jhonpra mosque. Its proximity to Pushkar adds to its significance as a spiritual hub.

Things To Do

  • Offer prayers and seek blessings at the Ajmer Sharif Dargah.
  • Enjoy a peaceful stroll or a boat ride on the Ana Sagar Lake.
  • Admire the unique Indo-Islamic architecture of Adhai Din Ka Jhonpra.
  • Explore the ruins and enjoy panoramic views from Taragarh Fort.
  • Visit the Akbari Fort and Museum for a glimpse into Mughal history.
  • Take a short trip to the nearby sacred town of Pushkar.

Location

Ajmer is located in central Rajasthan, approximately 135 km west of the state capital, Jaipur.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The annual Urs festival is a major event here.

Top Attractions

Ajmer Sharif Dargah Ana Sagar Lake Adhai Din Ka Jhonpra Taragarh Fort Nasiyan Jain Temple

How To Reach

By Air: Kishangarh Airport is about 30 km away. Jaipur Airport is the nearest major international airport.

By Train: Ajmer Junction is a major railway station with direct connectivity to all major cities.

By Road: Very well-connected by Rajasthan State Roadways and private buses to Jaipur, Delhi, and Udaipur.

Travel Tips

- Cover your head with a scarf or handkerchief when entering the Dargah.
- Be prepared for large crowds, especially during the Urs festival.
- Keep your belongings safe in crowded market areas.
- Try the local 'Sohan Halwa', a famous sweet of Ajmer.
