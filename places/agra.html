Overview

Agra is synonymous with the Taj Mahal, but this historical city has much more to offer. Located on the banks of the Yamuna River in Uttar Pradesh, Agra forms the famous Golden Triangle circuit along with Delhi and Jaipur. The city is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, housing three UNESCO World Heritage Sites: the Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, and Fatehpur Sikri. It is a bustling destination that beautifully blends history, culture, and magnificent monumental beauty.

Things To Do

  • Watch the sunrise at the Taj Mahal for breathtaking views and fewer crowds.
  • Explore the massive sandstone walls and palaces inside Agra Fort.
  • Take a day trip to the ghost city of Fatehpur Sikri.
  • Shop for exquisite marble inlay work, leather goods, and traditional rugs at Sadar Bazaar.
  • Taste the famous Agra Petha (a sweet made from pumpkin) and savory Dalmoth.
  • View the Taj Mahal from Mehtab Bagh at sunset across the river Yamuna.

Location

Agra is situated in the northern state of Uttar Pradesh, roughly 210 kilometers south of New Delhi.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Agra is during the winter season, between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and perfect for sightseeing.

Top Attractions

How To Reach

By Air: Agra has a military airbase, but the nearest commercial airport is in New Delhi (DEL).

By Train: Agra Cantt is well-connected to major Indian cities. The Gatimaan Express offers high-speed travel from Delhi.

By Road: The Yamuna Expressway forms a fast and scenic route from Delhi to Agra.

Travel Tips

- The Taj Mahal is closed on Fridays.
- Book tickets for monuments online to avoid long queues.
- Beware of unregulated guides and touts; hire official guides.
- Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
