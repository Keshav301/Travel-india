Overview

Chitrakoot, the 'Hill of Many Wonders', is a place of deep religious and cultural significance situated on the borders of Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh. It is here that Lord Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana are believed to have spent eleven and a half years of their fourteen-year exile. This tranquil town, dotted with numerous temples and natural beauty, is a major pilgrimage center. The serene Mandakini River and the lush green forests of the Vindhya Range provide a perfect backdrop for spiritual reflection.

Things To Do

  • Witness the evening aarti at Ramghat on the banks of Mandakini River.
  • Perform the 5-km Parikrama of the sacred Kamadgiri Hill.
  • Explore the mysterious and beautiful Gupt Godavari caves with their underground streams.
  • Visit the Sati Anusuya Ashram, a peaceful retreat deep in the forest.
  • Admire the waterfall and the footprint of Lord Rama at Hanuman Dhara.
  • Take a boat ride in the Mandakini River during sunset for a tranquil experience.

Location

Chitrakoot is located in the Bundelkhand region, spread across Chitrakoot district in Uttar Pradesh and Satna district in Madhya Pradesh.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Chitrakoot is from July to March. The monsoon month brings out the lush greenery, while winters are very pleasant.

How To Reach

By Air: Prayagraj Airport (IXD, 130 km) and Khajuraho Airport (HJR, 175 km) are the nearest.

By Train: Chitrakoot Dham Karwi (CKTD) is the major railway station, well-connected to Delhi, Mumbai, and Jabalpur.

By Road: Excellent bus services from Jhansi, Satna, Mahoba, and Prayagraj.

Travel Tips

- Wear comfortable shoes as there is a fair amount of walking and climbing (especially at Hanuman Dhara).
- Respect the spiritual sanctity; avoid loud music or disruptive behavior.
- Carry mosquito repellent if staying near the river or forest areas.
- The town is less commercialized, so carry sufficient cash.
