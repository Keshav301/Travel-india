Overview

Anjuna Beach is the heart of Goa's hippie culture and bohemian lifestyle. Famous since the 1960s, it's known for its laid-back vibe, trance parties, and the iconic Wednesday Flea Market. The beach itself is unique, with its red laterite cliffs and black volcanic rocks providing a dramatic backdrop to the shimmering Arabian Sea. Anjuna is a place where travelers from all over the world come to relax, explore, and soak in the vibrant, eclectic spirit of Goa.

Things To Do

  • Browse through the colorful stalls at the Wednesday Anjuna Flea Market.
  • Enjoy some live music and trance vibes at the beach-side shacks like Curlies.
  • Witness a stunning sunset while sipping a drink at a cliff-side cafe.
  • Explore the nearby Chapora Fort for panoramic views of Vagator and Anjuna.
  • Indulge in global cuisines and organic food at the many eclectic restaurants.
  • Join an early morning yoga or meditation session on the beach.

Location

Anjuna is located in North Goa, about 8 km from Mapusa and 18 km from Panaji.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from November to March. The Wednesday Flea Market is a must-visit during this time.

Top Attractions

Anjuna Flea Market Curlies Beach Shack Shiva Valley German Bakery Albuquerque Mansion

How To Reach

By Air: Dabolim Airport (45 km) or Mopa Airport (28 km).

By Train: Thivim is the nearest railway station (approx. 19 km).

By Road: Well-connected; the beach is easily accessible by taxis and rented vehicles.

Travel Tips

- Visit the Flea Market early in the morning to avoid the heat and get the best deals.
- Be careful when walking on the rocks, especially during high tide.
- Keep an open mind and embrace the eclectic, multi-cultural vibe of Anjuna.
- Try the local 'Feni' cautiously and enjoy the authentic Goan seafood.
