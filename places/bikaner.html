Overview

Bikaner, a vibrant desert city in the heart of Rajasthan, was founded by Rao Bika in 1488. It's famous for its magnificent Junagarh Fort, which uniquely sits on the plains rather than a hilltop. The city is a major center for fine arts and handicrafts, particularly the intricate Usta art (embossing on camel hide). Bikaner is also globally renowned for its salty snack, 'Bikaneri Bhujia', and its unique Karni Mata Temple, where thousands of rats are worshipped as sacred beings.

Things To Do

  • Explore the opulent palaces and museums within Junagarh Fort.
  • Visit the unique Karni Mata Temple (Rat Temple) in nearby Deshnok.
  • Tour the National Research Centre on Camel, the largest of its kind in Asia.
  • Marvel at the beautiful architecture of Lalgarh Palace.
  • Shop for authentic Bikaneri Bhujia, sweets, and handicrafts in the local markets.
  • Take a camel safari into the surrounding Thar Desert for a rustic experience.

Location

Bikaner is located in northwestern Rajasthan, surrounded by the Thar Desert.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The International Camel Festival in January is a major highlight.

Top Attractions

Junagarh Fort Karni Mata Temple Lalgarh Palace National Camel Research Centre Rampuria Haveli

How To Reach

By Air: Bikaner has its own domestic airport (BKB) with direct flights from Delhi and Jaipur.

By Train: Bikaner Railway Station is well-connected to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata.

By Road: Excellent highway connectivity; daily buses from Delhi, Jaipur, and Jodhpur.

Travel Tips

- Try the spicy 'Bikaner Bhujia' and 'Gund ke Laddu' when you visit.
- Wear comfortable cotton clothing for the desert weather.
- Carry socks when visiting the Karni Mata Temple, as you must remove your shoes.
- Explore the old city in a rickshaw to see the intricate Havelis.
