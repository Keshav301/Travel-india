Overview

Ranthambore National Park is one of the biggest and most renowned national parks in Northern India. Located in the Sawai Madhopur district of Rajasthan, it was once the private hunting grounds of the Maharajas of Jaipur. Today, it is a premier tiger reserve, famous globally for its diurnal Bengal tigers that are frequently spotted even during the day. The park is uniquely situated at the junction of the Aravalli and Vindhya hill ranges, and houses the formidable 10th-century Ranthambore Fort, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Things To Do

  • Go on an early morning or afternoon Jungle Safari (Jeep or Canter).
  • Hike up to the Ranthambore Fort for panoramic park views.
  • Visit the Trinetra Ganesha Temple, one of the oldest in Rajasthan.
  • Admire the beauty of Padam Talao, the largest lake in the park.
  • Explore the nearby village for local handicrafts and Rajasthani culture.
  • Bird watching around the various lakes and water bodies.

Location

Ranthambore is located in the Sawai Madhopur district of Rajasthan, about 160 km from Jaipur.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to June. The park remains closed during the monsoon (July-September).

Top Attractions

Tiger Sightings Ranthambore Fort Trinetra Ganesh Temple Padam Talao Raj Bagh Ruins

How To Reach

By Air: Jaipur International Airport is the closest (approx. 180 km).

By Train: Sawai Madhopur Railway Station is the nearest railhead, well-connected to major cities.

By Road: Very well-connected by road; several buses and taxis available from Jaipur and Delhi.

Travel Tips

- Book your safaris well in advance (months ahead for peak periods).
- Wear dull-colored clothing (khaki, olive, brown) to blend with the forest.
- Carry binoculars and a good camera for wildlife spotting.
- Maintain silence and follow the forest department's rules during safaris.
