Overview

Sivasagar, formerly known as Rangpur, was the capital of the mighty Ahom Kingdom for over a century. A city of massive tanks and historic monuments, it stands as a testament to the architectural brilliance of the Ahom dynasty. The city is named after the huge Sivasagar tank, which is flanked by the towering Shiva Dol, Vishnu Dol, and Devi Dol. Monuments like the Rang Ghar (an amphitheater) and the multi-storied Talatal Ghar showcase the fusion of indigenous techniques with broader influences, making Sivasagar a must-visit for history enthusiasts.

Things To Do

  • Explore the Rang Ghar, one of the oldest surviving amphitheaters in Asia.
  • Wander through the underground passages and levels of the Talatal Ghar.
  • Climb up to the Kareng Ghar, the multi-storied royal palace built for Ahom kings.
  • Visit the Sivasagar Sivasthan and see the massive Shiva Dol temple dome.
  • Take a relaxing walk around the banks of the Joysagar tank, the largest man-made tank in India.
  • Visit the Charaideo Maidams, the royal burial grounds known as the "Pyramids of Assam."

Location

Sivasagar is a district headquarters in Upper Assam, located about 360 km from Guwahati.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The Shivaratri fair at the Shiva Dol is a major event.

Top Attractions

How To Reach

By Air: Jorhat (75 km) and Dibrugarh (80 km) are the nearest airports.

By Train: Simaluguri Junction (16 km) is the nearest railhead.

By Road: Well-connected via NH-37. Regular luxury buses ply from Guwahati.

Travel Tips

- Hiring a local guide at the Talatal Ghar will help you understand the complex history of the Ahom kings.
- Wear comfortable walking shoes to explore the extensive monument grounds.
- Respect the religious sentiments when entering the Dol temple complexes.
- Don't miss out on trying local pithas (rice cakes) in the local markets.
