Overview

Jhansi, the gateway to Bundelkhand, is a city immortalized by the valor of its queen, Rani Lakshmibai. It was a key site during the 1857 First War of Independence. The city is built around the colossal Jhansi Fort, which sits majestically on a rocky hill. Jhansi is not just a place of historical significance; it is a symbol of Indian courage and resistance. Its heritage monuments, royal palaces, and brave stories make it a must-visit for every history enthusiast.

Things To Do

  • Explore the massive Jhansi Fort and see the spot from where Rani Lakshmibai jumped on her horse.
  • Visit the Rani Mahal, the former residence of the queen, famous for its exquisite wall paintings.
  • Pay your respects at the Rani Lakshmibai Park and its grand bronze statue.
  • Discover the history of the region at the Government Museum.
  • Take a day trip to the temple town of Orchha (just 15 km away).
  • Witness the spectacular Sound and Light Show at the Jhansi Fort in the evening.

Location

Jhansi is located in the extreme south of Uttar Pradesh, in the Bundelkhand region.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Jhansi is from October to March when the weather is pleasant for sightseeing.

Top Attractions

Jhansi Fort Rani Mahal Lakshmibai Park Ganesh Temple St. Jude's Shrine

How To Reach

By Air: Nearest airport is Gwalior (GWL, 100 km) or Khajuraho (HJR, 175 km).

By Train: Jhansi Junction is a major railway hub, excellently connected to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Chennai.

By Road: Well-connected by National Highways to Gwalior, Kanpur, and Bhopal.

Travel Tips

- Wear comfortable walking shoes for exploring the large fort area.
- Hire a local guide at the fort to hear the legendary tales of Rani Lakshmibai.
- Avoid visiting in peak summer (April-June) as it can be extremely hot.
- Combine your visit with Orchha for a complete Bundelkhand experience.
