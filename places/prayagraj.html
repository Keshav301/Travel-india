Overview

Prayagraj, formerly known as Allahabad, is one of the oldest and most sacred cities in India. It is uniquely situated at the "Triveni Sangam," the triple confluence of the Ganga, Yamuna, and the mythical subterranean Saraswati river. This makes it the ultimate destination for millions of pilgrims, especially during the Kumbh Mela, the largest spiritual gathering on Earth. Beyond its spiritual significance, Prayagraj served as a focal point during India's freedom struggle and is home to grand colonial architecture and prestigious institutions.

Things To Do

  • Take a boat ride to the exact point of the Triveni Sangam for a holy dip.
  • Explore the massive Allahabad Fort, built by Emperor Akbar in 1583.
  • Visit Anand Bhavan, the ancestral home of the Nehru-Gandhi family.
  • Walk through the beautiful Alfred Park (Company Bagh), where Chandra Shekhar Azad attained martyrdom.
  • Admire the Victorian-Gothic architecture of the Allahabad High Court.
  • Cross the New Yamuna Bridge for a panoramic view of the city skyline.

Location

Prayagraj is located in the southern part of Uttar Pradesh, at the confluence of the Ganga and Yamuna rivers.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is during the winter months, from October to March. The Magh Mela (Jan-Feb) is a great time to witness the city's religious fervor.

Top Attractions

Triveni Sangam Allahabad Fort Anand Bhavan Khusro Bagh All Saints Cathedral

How To Reach

By Air: Prayagraj Airport (IXD) offers direct flights to major Indian cities.

By Train: Prayagraj Junction (PRYJ) is a major hub on the Delhi-Kolkata and Mumbai-Howrah routes.

By Road: Well-connected by highways to Varanasi, Lucknow, and Kanpur.

Travel Tips

- Negotiate boat rates at the Sangam beforehand.
- Visit the Akshayavat (immortal banyan tree) inside the fort.
- Try the famous 'Allahabadi Guava' in season.
- Be prepared for massive crowds if visiting during a Mela.
