Overview

Mount Abu, the only hill station in the desert state of Rajasthan, is a verdant oasis located in the Aravalli Range. It offers a cool and refreshing escape from the sweltering heat of the plains. The town is most famous for the Dilwara Jain Temples, a complex of five temples renowned for their incredibly intricate marble carvings. Nakki Lake, the first man-made lake in India, sits at the heart of the town, surrounded by local markets and scenic points like Toad Rock.

Things To Do

  • Marvel at the stunning marble architecture of the Dilwara Jain Temples.
  • Enjoy a relaxed boat ride on Nakki Lake.
  • Take a short trek to Guru Shikhar, the highest point in Rajasthan.
  • Witness a spectacular sunset at the Sunset Point.
  • Hike to Toad Rock for a unique perspective of the lake.
  • Explore the Mount Abu Wildlife Sanctuary for diverse flora and fauna.

Location

Mount Abu is located in the Sirohi district of Rajasthan, near the border with Gujarat.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from November to June. It's also a popular destination during the monsoon (July-September).

Top Attractions

Dilwara Temples Nakki Lake Guru Shikhar Sunset Point Achalgarh Fort

How To Reach

By Air: Udaipur Airport is the nearest (approx. 185 km), while Ahmedabad Airport (Gujarat) is about 220 km away.

By Train: Abu Road is the nearest railway station (approx. 28 km), well-connected to major cities.

By Road: Very well-connected by road; luxury buses operate from Jaipur, Udaipur, and Ahmedabad.

Travel Tips

- Carry a light sweater or jacket, even in summer, as evenings can be cool.
- Photography is strictly prohibited inside the Dilwara Temples.
- Be careful of monkeys around Nakki Lake and the Sunset Point.
- Book your hotel in advance during peak season and weekends.
