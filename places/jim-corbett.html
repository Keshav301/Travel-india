Overview

Jim Corbett National Park, established in 1936 as Hailey National Park, is India's oldest national park and protected area. Named after the legendary hunter-turned-conservationist Jim Corbett, the park is famous for being the birthplace of Project Tiger. It is known for its diverse wildlife, including Bengal tigers, leopards, and over 600 species of birds. The park's varied landscape includes hills, riverine belts, marshy depressions, grasslands, and a large lake, making it a paradise for nature lovers and wildlife photography enthusiasts.

Things To Do

  • Experience a thrilling Jeep Safari in the core zones of the park.
  • Enjoy an Elephant Safari for a unique perspective on the jungle wildlife.
  • Explore the Dhikala zone for its rich biodiversity and scenic views.
  • Go birdwatching and spot rare avian species along the Ramganga.
  • Visit the Corbett Museum in Kaladhungi, once the home of Jim Corbett.
  • Stay at the forest rest houses inside the park for an immersive wilderness experience.

Location

Jim Corbett National Park is located in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand, near the town of Ramnagar.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from November to June. The park remains closed during the monsoon season (July to October) for wildlife safety.

Top Attractions

Dhikala Zone Bijrani Zone Corbett Falls Garjiya Devi Temple Ramganga Reservoir

How To Reach

By Air: Pantnagar Airport is the nearest (80 km).

By Train: Ramnagar Railway Station is the nearest railhead, well-linked to Delhi.

By Road: Excellent connectivity to Delhi (approx. 5-6 hours drive) and Nainital.

Travel Tips

- Safari permits are issued on a first-come, first-served basis; book in advance online.
- Carry earth-toned clothing (greens, browns) to blend into the forest environment.
- Do not litter or make loud noises inside the park; respect the silence of the wild.
- Maintain a safe distance from wild animals and follow all guidelines from your guide.
