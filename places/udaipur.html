Overview

Often called the "Venice of the East," Udaipur is a stunning city centered around a series of artificial lakes. Founded by Maharana Udai Singh II in 1559, it served as the historic capital of the kingdom of Mewar. The city is famed for its romantic atmosphere, opulent palaces, and heritage havelis. The massive City Palace complex, overlooking Lake Pichola, is a marvel of Rajput architecture and remains the heart of the city's cultural life.

Things To Do

  • Take a boat ride on Lake Pichola for breathtaking views of the palaces.
  • Explore the vast museums and courtyards of the City Palace.
  • Visit the Jag Mandir island palace for a tranquil experience.
  • Witness a traditional Bagore Ki Haveli puppet show and folk dance.
  • Drive up to the Sajjangarh (Monsoon) Palace for a panoramic sunset.
  • Walk through the colorful markets for authentic Rajasthani paintings and crafts.

Location

Udaipur is located in the southern part of Rajasthan, nestled among the Aravalli Range.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from September to March. The monsoon also makes the city exceptionally green and beautiful.

Top Attractions

Lake Pichola City Palace Jag Mandir Saheliyon Ki Bari Sajjangarh Palace

How To Reach

By Air: Maharana Pratap Airport (UDR) is about 22 km from the city center.

By Train: Udaipur City Railway Station is well-connected to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Jaipur.

By Road: Udaipur is connected via NH48, with regular bus services from neighboring states.

Travel Tips

- Book boat rides in advance during the peak tourist season.
- Carry light cotton clothes for the day and a light jacket for winter nights.
- Explore the old city on foot or via rickshaws as streets can be narrow.
- Try the traditional Lal Maas (spicy mutton curry) if you enjoy non-veg food.
