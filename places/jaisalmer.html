Overview

Jaisalmer, known as the "Golden City," is heart of the Great Indian Desert (Thar Desert). The city gets its name from the golden-yellow sandstone used in almost every architecture, which gives the city a magical glow when the sun shines. The Jaisalmer Fort, unlike many other forts, is a living heritage where a significant portion of the city's population still resides. Founded in 1156 AD, the city's history is steeped in tales of trade and chivalry along the ancient Silk Road route.

Things To Do

  • Explore the narrow lanes and Jain temples within the Jaisalmer Fort.
  • Experience a Camel Safari and overnight camping at Sam Sand Dunes.
  • Admire the intricate stone carvings and architecture of the Havelis.
  • Take a boat ride in the Gadisar Lake during sunset.
  • Visit the Kuldhara Village - a mysterious abandoned "ghost village."
  • Try Rajasthani specialties like Ker Sangri and Gatte ki Sabzi.

Location

Jaisalmer is located in the far western part of Rajasthan, close to the border with Pakistan.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from November to February. The desert heat is unbearable during other months.

Top Attractions

Jaisalmer Fort Sam Sand Dunes Patwon Ki Haveli Gadisar Lake Desert National Park

How To Reach

By Air: Jaisalmer Airport has seasonal flights to major cities like Delhi and Jaipur.

By Train: Jaisalmer Railway Station is well-connected to Jodhpur, Delhi, and Jaipur.

By Road: Long-distance buses and luxury taxis are available from Jodhpur (approx. 5 hours).

Travel Tips

- Carry adequate sunscreen, sunglasses, and head covers for the desert safari.
- Stay hydrated and carry enough water during desert excursions.
- Respect local customs and traditions when visiting temples.
- Avoid desert safaris during peak noon hours to prevent heat exhaustion.
