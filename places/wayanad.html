Overview

Wayanad is a serene, rural district in Kerala, tucked away in the Western Ghats. It's a land of misty mountains, lush forests, and sprawling spice plantations. Wayanad is famous for its diverse wildlife, ancient caves with prehistoric petroglyphs, and breathtaking waterfalls. It offers a perfect blend of adventure and tranquility, making it a favorite for trekkers, nature lovers, and those seeking a quiet escape.

Things To Do

  • Trek to the heart-shaped Lake at Chembra Peak.
  • Explore the ancient Edakkal Caves and admire the rock carvings.
  • Take a wildlife safari in the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Visit the Banasura Sagar Dam, India's largest earth dam.
  • Enjoy a refreshing dip at Soochipara (Sentinel Rock) Waterfalls.
  • Tour the coffee and spice plantations to see how they are grown.

Location

Wayanad is located in the northern part of Kerala, bordered by Karnataka and Tamil Nadu.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to May. The monsoon (June-August) transforms the region into an emerald paradise.

Top Attractions

Banasura Sagar Dam Edakkal Caves Chembra Peak Pookode Lake Kuruvadweep

How To Reach

By Air: Calicut International Airport is the nearest (approx. 95 km).

By Train: Kozhikode (Calicut) is the closest major railway station.

By Road: Very well-connected by road; scenic drives through forest ghats from Kozhikode and Mysore.

Travel Tips

- Carry light woolens, especially if you are visiting during winter.
- Wear comfortable trekking shoes for caves and waterfall visits.
- Be mindful of leeches when walking in forest areas, especially during monsoon.
- Respect the natural environment and avoid littering in the wildlife zones.
