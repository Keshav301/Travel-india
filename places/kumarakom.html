Overview

Kumarakom is a cluster of little islands on the Vembanad Lake, Kerala's largest lake. It is part of the Kuttanad region and is world-famous for its backwater tourism. The Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, spread over 14 acres, is a favorite haunt for migratory birds and an ornithologist's paradise. The region's lush greenery, tranquil waters, and premium luxury resorts make it a perfect destination for those seeking peace and a deep connection with nature.

Things To Do

  • Take an early morning walk through the Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary.
  • Enjoy a relaxing houseboat cruise on the vast Vembanad Lake.
  • Indulge in a sunset motorboat ride for stunning water views.
  • Try your hand at local fishing or 'Toddy tapping' demonstrations.
  • Pamper yourself with world-class Ayurvedic spa treatments at a resort.
  • Explore the local village on a bicycle to see its authentic beauty.

Location

Kumarakom is located in the Kottayam district of Kerala, on the banks of Vembanad Lake.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from September to March. The migratory bird season (November to February) is a special highlight.

Top Attractions

Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary Vembanad Lake Pathiramanal Island Bay Island Driftwood Museum Aruvikkuzhi Waterfall

How To Reach

By Air: Cochin International Airport is the nearest (approx. 85 km).

By Train: Kottayam Railway Station is the nearest railhead (approx. 16 km away).

By Road: Very well-connected by road to Kottayam, Kochi, and Alleppey.

Travel Tips

- Carry mosquito repellent when visiting the bird sanctuary and backwaters.
- Use a high-zoom lens or binoculars for bird watching.
- Try the local 'Karimeen' (Pearl Spot fish) and other backwater delicacies.
- Book your resort in advance as Kumarakom is a premium, high-demand destination.
