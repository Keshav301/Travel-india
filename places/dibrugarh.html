Overview

Dibrugarh, the "Tea City of India," is a major city in Upper Assam and the largest exporter of tea in the country. Situated on the banks of the Brahmaputra, it is a city of immense natural beauty and industrial importance. Known for its vast, well-maintained tea estates that date back to the British era, Dibrugarh is also a hub for oil and gas. The city offers a unique blend of urban lifestyle and rural charm, with the majestic river defining its landscape and providing a backdrop for some of the most stunning sunsets in the Northeast.

Things To Do

  • Take a "Tea Walk" through some of the world's most beautiful tea estates.
  • Enjoy an evening on the Brahmaputra riverfront and watch the sunset over the water.
  • Cross the Bogibeel Bridge for a panoramic view of the river and the plains.
  • Visit the Dehing Patkai Wildlife Sanctuary, often called the "Amazon of the East."
  • Explore the local Xatiya marketplace for authentic Assamese handloom and bell-metal products.
  • Take an excursion to Digboi, the oldest oil city, or join a tea factory tour.

Location

Dibrugarh is the headquarters of Dibrugarh district in Upper Assam, Northeast India.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The Dehing Patkai Festival in January is a major highlight.

Top Attractions

Tea Plantations Brahmaputra River Bogibeel Bridge Dehing Patkai Jokai Botanical Garden

How To Reach

By Air: Dibrugarh Airport (Mohanbari) offers regular flights from Delhi, Kolkata, and Guwahati.

By Train: Dibrugarh Railway Station is a major junction, connected by Rajdhani and other superfast trains.

By Road: Very well-connected by NH-37. Regular deluxe buses available from Guwahati and Itanagar.

Travel Tips

- Try the local "Ooti" dish and smoked fish delicacies.
- Carry light woolens during winter and a sturdy umbrella during the monsoon months.
- If you're a nature lover, plan at least two days for the Dehing Patkai sanctuary exploration.
- Interaction with tea garden communities offers a deep insight into the local lifestyle.
