Overview

Digboi, often called the "Oil City of Assam," holds a unique place in history as the birthplace of the Indian oil industry. In the late 19th century, oil was discovered here, leading to the establishment of the oldest operating oil refinery in the world. The town's colonial past is beautifully preserved in its British-style bungalows, clubs, and heritage museums. Beyond its industry, Digboi is a city of natural beauty, featuring a world-class 18-hole golf course surrounded by lush tea gardens and a poignant World War II cemetery that honors soldiers who fought in the region.

Things To Do

  • Step back in time at the Digboi Centenary Oil Museum to see vintage machinery and chronicles of the oil discovery.
  • Pay your respects at the beautifully maintained World War II War Cemetery, overseen by the CWGC.
  • Take a guided walk near the Digboi Oil Refinery to admire the industrial heritage (inner access limited).
  • Play a round of golf at the Digboi Golf Course, one of the most scenic in Asia.
  • Spend a peaceful afternoon at the Digboi Lake, a popular local spot for relaxation.
  • Visit the Dibru-Saikhowa National Park (50 km away), famous for its feral horses and diverse bird species.

Location

Digboi is a historic town in the Tinsukia district of Upper Assam.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March when the weather is cool and clear.

How To Reach

By Air: Dibrugarh Airport (approx. 80 km) is the nearest major airport.

By Train: Tinsukia (approx. 33 km) is the nearest major rail hub; Digboi has a small local station.

By Road: Well-connected through NH-37 with regular public transport from Tinsukia and Dibrugarh.

Travel Tips

- Most heritage museums have strict timings, so check before visiting.
- It's a fantastic place for those into industrial photography and historical architecture.
- The town is safe and walkable; take a morning stroll through the residential colonial quarters.
- Try local forest honey and handmade pickles in the local market.
