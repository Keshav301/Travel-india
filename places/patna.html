Overview

Patna, the capital of Bihar, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited places in the world. Originally known as Pataliputra, it was the capital of the great Mauryan and Gupta empires. Situated on the southern bank of the holy Ganges, Patna is a city where history and modernity coexist. From the massive Golghar granary built during the British era to the sacred Takht Sri Patna Sahib (birthplace of Guru Gobind Singh Ji) and the world-class Bihar Museum, the city offers a rich tapestry of experiences for history buffs and spiritual seekers alike.

Things To Do

  • Climb to the top of Golghar for a panoramic view of the city and the Ganges.
  • Explore the vast collections of ancient artifacts at the Bihar Museum.
  • Pay your respects at Takht Sri Patna Sahib, one of the five takhts of Sikhism.
  • Take a sunset walk along the Marine Drive (Ganga Path) for beautiful river views.
  • Visit the Patna Museum to see the famous Didarganj Yakshi and other Mauryan treasures.
  • Explore the ruins of ancient Pataliputra at the Kumhrar archeological site.

Location

Patna is located on the southern bank of the River Ganga in central Bihar.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The Chhath Puja festival in late autumn is a spectacular time to witness local culture.

Top Attractions

Bihar Museum Takht Sri Patna Sahib Golghar Patna Museum Gandhi Ghat

How To Reach

By Air: Jay Prakash Narayan International Airport (PAT) is well-connected to major Indian cities.

By Train: Patna Junction is a major railway hub on the Delhi-Kolkata main line.

By Road: Very well-connected by National Highways; regular bus services to neighboring states.

Travel Tips

- Traffic can be heavy in old Patna; plan your travel time accordingly.
- Dedicate at least 3-4 hours to the Bihar Museum to fully appreciate its collection.
- Use authorized taxis or ride-sharing apps for commuting within the city.
- Taste the local street food like Litti Chokha at reputable outlets.
