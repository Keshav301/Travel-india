Overview

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is known as the "Pink City" for its trademark terracotta-colored buildings. Founded by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II in 1727, it's India's first planned city. Part of the Golden Triangle tourist circuit, Jaipur is a vibrant blend of history and modernity, featuring magnificent hill forts, opulent palaces, and bustling traditional markets. The city is a UNESCO World Heritage site and remains a bastion of Rajput culture and royal heritage.

Things To Do

  • Take an elephant ride or jeep safari up to the Amer Fort.
  • Marvel at the intricate lattice windows of the Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds).
  • Explore the royal collection and courtyards at the City Palace.
  • Witness the world's largest stone sundial at Jantar Mantar.
  • Shop for traditional Jaipuri jewelry, textiles, and handicrafts in Johari Bazaar.
  • Enjoy a sunset view of the city from Nahargarh Fort.

Location

Jaipur is located in the eastern part of Rajasthan, approximately 280 km from the national capital, Delhi.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March when the weather is pleasant for sightseeing.

Top Attractions

Hawa Mahal Amer Fort City Palace Jal Mahal Nahargarh Fort

How To Reach

By Air: Jaipur International Airport (JAI) is well-connected to major Indian and international cities.

By Train: Jaipur Junction is a major railway hub with direct trains from all over India.

By Road: Excellent connectivity via NH48; several luxury buses ply from Delhi and Agra.

Travel Tips

- Wear comfortable walking shoes for exploring the forts.
- Hire certified guides at monuments to understand the rich history.
- Be prepared to bargain in the local bazaars.
- Carry a hat and sunscreen, as the sun can be strong even in winter.
