Overview

Gaya is one of the most important spiritual hubs in India, particularly for Hindus who visit the city to perform the 'Pind Daan' ritual for their ancestors' salvation. Located on the banks of the Falgu River, the city is holy to both Hindus and Buddhists (with Bodh Gaya just 12 km away). The most famous landmark is the Vishnupad Temple, situated by the river, which is said to house the footprint of Lord Vishnu on a solid rock. The city finds mention in the Ramayana and Mahabharata, as well as in the records of ancient travelers.

Things To Do

  • Visit the Vishnupad Temple and admire its 18th-century architecture and sacred footprints.
  • Participate in or observe the Pind Daan rituals on the ghats of the Falgu River.
  • Climb the 1,000 steps of Brahmajuni Hill for a panoramic view of the city and surroundings.
  • Visit the Mangla Gauri Temple, an important Shakti Peetha for Hindu devotees.
  • Explore the ancient caves and temples in the nearby Barabar Caves complex.
  • Witness the vibrant atmosphere during the Pitrupaksha Mela held annually in September/October.

Location

Gaya is located in South Bihar, situated about 100 km from the state capital, Patna.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The Pitrupaksha Mela (Sept/Oct) is a major religious period but can be very crowded.

Top Attractions

Vishnupad Temple Falgu River Ghats Mangla Gauri Temple Brahmajuni Hill Dungeshwari Caves

How To Reach

By Air: Gaya International Airport is very close; Patna Airport (100 km) is the nearest major hub.

By Train: Gaya Junction is a major railway station well-connected to Delhi, Kolkata, and Mumbai.

By Road: Very well-connected by road; regular buses operate from Patna, Varanasi, and Ranchi.

Travel Tips

- Be wary of touts and unverified priests at the river ghats; it's best to pre-arrange any rituals through reputable sources.
- Wear comfortable cotton clothing, especially if visiting during the warmer months.
- Respect the solemnity of the funeral rituals being performed at the riverfront.
- Carry change of clothes if you plan on taking a dip in the holy river.
