Overview

Pushkar, one of the oldest existing cities in India, is a sacred town centered around the holy Pushkar Lake. It is one of the five sacred dhams for devout Hindus and is home to the only temple in the world dedicated to Lord Brahma. The town is famous globally for the Pushkar Camel Fair, held annually in November, which attracts thousands of tourists and livestock traders. With its 52 ghats and hundreds of temples, Pushkar exudes a unique spiritual and hippie vibe that makes it a must-visit destination.

Things To Do

  • Take a holy dip in the Pushkar Lake and witness the evening aarti.
  • Visit the Brahma Temple, an architectural and spiritual marvel.
  • Explore the vibrant Pushkar market for silver jewelry and hippie clothing.
  • Hike up to the Savitri Temple for a panoramic view of the town.
  • Attend the Camel Fair (if visiting in November) for a cultural extravaganza.
  • Enjoy a desert safari on a camel or in a jeep.

Location

Pushkar is located in the Ajmer district of Rajasthan, about 15 km from Ajmer city, separated by the Nag Pahar.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The Camel Fair usually takes place in October or November.

Top Attractions

Pushkar Lake Brahma Temple Savitri Temple Varaha Temple Rangji Temple

How To Reach

By Air: Kishangarh Airport (near Ajmer) is the nearest, followed by Jaipur Airport (approx. 150 km).

By Train: Ajmer Junction is the nearest major railhead, well-connected to all major cities.

By Road: Pushkar is well-connected by road to Ajmer, Jaipur, and Delhi.

Travel Tips

- Pushkar is a strictly vegetarian and alcohol-free town.
- Be wary of "Pushkar priests" who might pressure you for donations.
- Dress modestly when visiting the lake and temples.
- Remove shoes before entering any ghat or temple area.
