Overview

Nalanda was an ancient Mahavihara (large Buddhist monastery) and a renowned center of learning in the kingdom of Magadha. Active from the 5th century CE to 1200 CE, it is considered the first residential university in the world, once hosting over 10,000 students and 2,000 teachers from across Asia. The UNESCO-listed archaeological site today showcases the impressive red-brick ruins of temples and monasteries, providing a glimpse into the sophisticated architecture and intellectual excellence of ancient India. The site was famously documented by the Chinese traveler Xuanzang.

Things To Do

  • Walk through the excavation site and admire the symmetry of the ancient monasteries.
  • Visit the Nalanda Archaeological Museum to see statues, coins, and seals found on-site.
  • Explore the Xuanzang Memorial Hall, built in honor of the famous Chinese monk and scholar.
  • Admire the Sariputra Stupa with its distinctive terraced architecture and votive stupas.
  • Visit the nearby Nalanda University (Modern) to see how the legacy of learning is being revived.
  • Take a quiet moment at the Hieun Tsang Memorial Hall surrounded by beautiful gardens.

Location

Nalanda is situated in the Nalanda district, about 90 km southeast of Patna and 15 km from Rajgir.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The weather is pleasant for walking through the extensive ruins.

Top Attractions

Archeological Site of Nalanda Sariputra Stupa Nalanda Museum Xuanzang Memorial Hall Kundalpur (Nearby Digambar Jain Site)

How To Reach

By Air: Patna Airport (approx. 90 km) is the nearest major airport.

By Train: Nalanda has a local station, but Rajgir (15 km) and Bihar Sharif (13 km) are better-connected hubs.

By Road: Very well-connected by State Highways to Patna and Rajgir; regular buses and taxis available.

Travel Tips

- Wear comfortable walking shoes as the site is large and requires a lot of walking.
- Hiring a government-approved guide is highly recommended to understand the layout and history.
- Carry a bottle of water and a hat as it can get sunny during the day.
- Respect the site and refrain from touching or climbing on the ancient brick walls.
