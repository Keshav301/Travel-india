Overview

Vaishali is a place of great historical and religious significance, credited as being the world's first republic with a duly elected assembly. It was the capital of the Licchavi state and is intertwined with the lives of both Lord Buddha and Lord Mahaveera. Buddha preached his last sermon here and announced his approaching Nirvana. For Jains, it is revered as the birthplace of Lord Mahaveera, the 24th Tirthankara. The town is famous for its remarkably well-preserved Ashoka Pillar, topped by a single lion, and the ancient relic stupa.

Things To Do

  • Visit the Ashoka Pillar at Kolhua and the surrounding monastic ruins.
  • Pay your respects at the Buddha Relic Stupa, one of the eight original reliquaries.
  • Explore the Vishwa Shanti Stupa, a towering white pagoda symbolising world peace.
  • Take a sacred dip or sit by the Abhishek Pushkarini, the coronation tank of ancient Lichchavi kings.
  • Visit the birthplace of Lord Mahaveera at Kundalpur (near Vaishali).
  • Learn about local history at the Vaishali Museum, which houses artifacts from the Mauryan and Kushan eras.

Location

Vaishali is located in the Vaishali district, approximately 55 km north of Patna.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The Vaishali Mahotsav (March/April) celebrating Mahaveer Jayanti is a major event.

How To Reach

By Air: Patna Airport (approx. 60 km) is the nearest airport.

By Train: Hajipur Junction (35 km) is the nearest major railway station.

By Road: Well-connected to Patna and Muzaffarpur via road; regular bus services available from Patna bus stand.

Travel Tips

- Vaishali is best explored as a day trip from Patna; start early in the morning.
- Carry sun protection (caps, sunglasses) as the archaeological sites are mostly open-air.
- Hiring a local guide at Kolhua will help you understand the complex history of the pillar and stupas.
- Respect the tranquil atmosphere of the religious sites.
