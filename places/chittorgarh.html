Overview

Chittorgarh, the former capital of the Mewar Kingdom, is home to the Chittorgarh Fort—the largest fort in India. Standing atop a 180-meter high hill, the fort is a symbol of Rajput bravery, sacrifice, and chivalry. Its history is marked by three legendary sieges and the supreme sacrifice of its people through Jauhar. The fort complex is massive, spanning 700 acres, and contains several magnificent towers, palaces, temples, and large water bodies that showcase the architectural brilliance of its time.

Things To Do

  • Explore the vast Chittorgarh Fort and its seven massive gateways.
  • Climb to the top of the Vijay Stambh (Tower of Victory) for fort views.
  • Visit the Padmini's Palace, where legend says Alauddin Khilji saw Queen Padmini.
  • Admire the intricate carvings on the Kirti Stambh (Tower of Fame).
  • Witness the serene Gaumukh Reservoir within the fort walls.
  • Attend the Light and Sound show in the evening for a dramatic history lesson.

Location

Chittorgarh is located in southern Rajasthan, on the banks of the Berach and Gambhiri rivers.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The weather is ideal for exploring the vast fort on foot.

Top Attractions

Chittorgarh Fort Vijay Stambh Padmini's Palace Kirti Stambh Gaumukh Reservoir

How To Reach

By Air: Maharana Pratap Airport (Udaipur) is the nearest, located about 90 km away.

By Train: Chittorgarh Railway Station is a major junction on the Western Railway network.

By Road: Very well-connected by highways to Udaipur, Jaipur, and Indore.

Travel Tips

- Hire a local guide or an auto-rickshaw to cover the vast fort area.
- Carry plenty of water and wear a hat as the fort area is very open.
- Respect the spiritual sanctity of the temples within the fort.
- Allow at least 4-5 hours to explore the major attractions of the fort.
