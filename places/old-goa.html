Overview

Old Goa (Velha Goa) was the former capital of Portuguese India from the 16th to the 18th centuries. Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage site, world-renowned for its architectural treasures and historic churches. The city's golden age is reflected in the massive Se Cathedral—the largest church in Asia, and the Basilica of Bom Jesus, which houses the sacred remains of St. Francis Xavier. Walking through Old Goa is like stepping back in time to the era of Portuguese dominance in the East.

Things To Do

  • Marvel at the sheer size and Portuguese-Gothic architecture of Se Cathedral.
  • Pay your respects at the tomb of St. Francis Xavier in the Basilica of Bom Jesus.
  • Explore the Archeological Museum of Goa for insights into the region's colonial history.
  • Photograph the stunning St. Augustine Tower, a lonely remnant of a grand church.
  • Admire the beauty of the Church of Our Lady of the Mount for panoramic views of the Mandovi River.
  • Take a slow walk through the peaceful, heritage-filled streets of the complex.

Location

Old Goa is located in North Goa district, about 10 km east of Panaji along the Mandovi River.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The Feast of St. Francis Xavier in early December is a major event.

Top Attractions

Basilica of Bom Jesus Se Cathedral Church of St. Cajetan St. Augustine Tower ASI Museum

How To Reach

By Air: Dabolim Airport (approx. 25 km) or Mopa Airport (approx. 40 km).

By Train: Karmali is the nearest railway station (approx. 2 km away).

By Road: Very well-connected by road; frequent buses and taxis from Panaji.

Travel Tips

- Dress modestly and respect the spiritual sanctity of the churches.
- Photography is allowed in most areas, but avoid using flash inside the churches.
- Hire a local guide for a more profound understanding of the historical significance.
- Wear comfortable shoes as you will be doing a fair amount of walking between the monuments.
