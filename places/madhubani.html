Overview

Madhubani, literally meaning "Forest of Honey," is a historic district in the Mithila region of Bihar. It is globaly renowned as the cradle of Madhubani (or Mithila) painting, a vibrant folk art form traditionally done by women on the walls of their homes. Every corner of the city and its surrounding villages like Jhitwarpur and Ranti feels like an open-air art gallery. Madhubani is also a land of poets and scholars, known for its deep-rooted cultural heritage and its unique contribution to India’s artistic landscape.

Things To Do

  • Visit Jhitwarpur and Ranti villages to meet National Award-winning artists and see live painting.
  • Take part in a Madhubani painting workshop to learn children of this ancient art.
  • Admire the walls of the Madhubani Railway Station, completely covered in vibrant murals.
  • Explore the Saurath Sabha Gachhi, a historic site where traditional Mithila marriages were once arranged.
  • Visit the Kapileshwar Sthan, a major Shiva temple and a site of religious fairs.
  • Shop for authentic Madhubani paintings, hand-painted sarees, and handicrafts directly from the sources.

Location

Madhubani is located in North Bihar, part of the Mithila region near the Nepal border.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The Chhath Puja and Vivah Panchami are great periods to see the local culture at its peak.

Top Attractions

Jhitwarpur Artist Village Madhubani Railway Station Kapileshwar Sthan Temple Saurath Sabha Ranti Village

How To Reach

By Air: Darbhanga Airport (approx. 35 km) is the nearest airport with good connectivity.

By Train: Madhubani has its own railway station connected to major cities like Delhi and Kolkata.

By Road: Well-connected by National Highways to Darbhanga, Patna, and Muzaffarpur.

Travel Tips

- Try to visit during a local festival to see the most authentic use of the folk art in rituals.
- Carry enough cash if visiting remote artist villages as digital payments might be limited.
- Respect the privacy of the artists when visiting their homes; always ask before taking photos.
- Buy paintings directly from artists to support the local community and get the best price.
