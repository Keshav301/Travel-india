Overview

Majuli, the world's largest river island, is the cultural capital of Assam. Nestled in the mighty Brahmaputra River, it is the center of the Neo-Vaishnavite movement, founded by the saint-reformer Srimanta Sankaradeva. The island is dotted with unique monasteries called Satras, where monks preserve ancient art forms, drama, music, and dance. Known for its breathtaking sunsets, lush wetlands, and vibrant tribal culture, Majuli offers an escape into a world of spiritual serenity and artistic richness.

Things To Do

  • Explore the historic Satras like Auniati, Kamalabari, and Garmur to learn about Vaishnavite traditions.
  • Visit the Samaguri Satra to witness the traditional art of mask-making used in Bhaona (theatrical performances).
  • Rent a bicycle and ride through the scenic trails, crossing bamboo bridges and traditional stilt houses.
  • Take an evening boat ride on the river to watch the spectacular sunset and migratory birds.
  • Watch a live performance of Sattriya dance, one of the eight classical dance forms of India.
  • Interact with the Mishing tribe and try their local rice wine (Apong) and cuisine.

Location

Majuli is an island district in Assam, accessible via ferry from Nimati Ghat near Jorhat.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The Raas Leela festival in November is a major highlight.

Top Attractions

Vaishnavite Satras Samaguri Mask Art Mishing Tribal Villages Kamalabari Satra Auniati Museum

How To Reach

By Air: Jorhat (approx. 20 km to ferry terminal) is the nearest airport.

By Train: Jorhat railway station is well-connected.

By Ferry: Regular ferries operate from Nimati Ghat (Jorhat) to Kamalabari/Afalamukh (Majuli).

Travel Tips

- Always check the ferry timings as they may change due to river levels.
- Carry mosquito repellent if you plan to stay in eco-camps or bamboo huts.
- Respect the sanctity of the Satras; remove footwear and seek permission for photos.
- Majuli is a plastic-free zone; ensure you do not litter and carry reusable bags.
