Overview

Auli, a premier skiing destination in India, is a breathtaking Himalayan retreat located at an altitude of 2,800 meters. Known for its sparkling slopes and clean environment, Auli is surrounded by coniferous and oak forests. It offers a panoramic view of the highest peaks of the Himalayas, including Nanda Devi, Kamet, and Mana Parvat. The town is famous for its long distance cable car (Ropeway) connecting it to Joshimath, offering some of the most spectacular aerial views of the mountain range.

Things To Do

  • Experience professional Skiing on the world-class slopes of Auli.
  • Ride the Joshimath-Auli Ropeway, one of the longest in Asia.
  • Visit the Auli Artificial Lake, one of the world's highest man-made lakes.
  • Trek to Gorson Bugyal for breathtaking meadow views.
  • Witness the panoramic view of the Himalayas from the Auli Chair Lift.
  • Visit the Chattrakund, a small lake known for its crystal clear waters.

Location

Auli is located in the Chamoli district of the Garhwal region in Uttarakhand.

Best Time to Visit

For skiing, visit between January and March. For pleasant weather and trekking, April to June and September to November are ideal.

Top Attractions

Auli Ropeway Auli Artificial Lake Gorson Bugyal Kuwari Pass Trishul Peak View

How To Reach

By Air: Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun is the nearest (280 km).

By Train: Rishikesh is the nearest railhead (250 km).

By Road: Bus or taxi from Rishikesh to Joshimath, then the ropeway or taxi to Auli.

Travel Tips

- Carry heavy woolens if visiting in winter; temperatures can drop significantly.
- Use sunblock; the high-altitude sun can be very harsh even in winter.
- Check the operation of the ropeway in advance, especially during the off-season.
- Accommodation is limited; book well in advance for the skiing season.
