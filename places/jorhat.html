Overview

Jorhat, the "Tea Capital of the World," is the vibrant heart of the Assam tea industry. Famed for its sprawling tea estates and colonial bungalows, it was once the last capital of the Ahom Kingdom. Jorhat is a hub of education and artistic pursuit, and it serves as the primary base for visiting Majuli. From the world's oldest tea research center to the rare Hoolock Gibbons in its nearby sanctuary, Jorhat blends industrial significance with natural beauty and historical pride.

Things To Do

  • Take a tour of a tea production factory to learn how the world's finest tea is processed.
  • Stay in a heritage tea bungalow for an authentic colonial planter's experience.
  • Visit the Hoollongapar Gibbon Sanctuary to spot India's only ape species in the wild.
  • Admire the historic architecture and golf course at the Jorhat Gymkhana Club.
  • Take a ferry from Nimati Ghat to explore the spiritual Satras of Majuli.
  • Wander through the Raja Maidam park, where the royal graves of Ahom kings are located.

Location

Jorhat is an important city in Upper Assam, located about 300 km east of Guwahati.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from November to March. The Tea Festival in January is a great cultural experience.

Top Attractions

Tea Estates Gibbon Sanctuary Tocklai Tea Research Gymkhana Club Nimati Ghat

How To Reach

By Air: Jorhat (Rowriah) Airport is located right in the city.

By Train: Jorhat Town and Mariani Junction are major railheads nearby.

By Road: Very well-connected by NH-37 to Guwahati, Dibrugarh, and Kaziranga.

Travel Tips

- Always buy your tea from official factory outlets to get the freshest and best quality.
- If you plan to visit the Gibbon Sanctuary, start early in the morning for the best sightings.
- Book your stay in a tea estate bungalow well in advance as they are very popular.
- Jorhat is a great place to buy traditional Assamese jewelry and textiles.
