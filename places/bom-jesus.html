Overview

The Basilica of Bom Jesus, located in Old Goa, is one of the most iconic and revered landmarks in India. Completed in 1605, it is a magnificent example of Jesuit and Baroque architecture. The basilica is world-famous for housing the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier, the patron saint of Goa, within a beautiful silver casket. It is the only church in Old Goa that has its exterior un-plastered, showing the beautiful red laterite stone. The basilica is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a prominent pilgrimage center for Christians worldwide.

Things To Do

  • Admire the grandeur of the main altar, gilded with gold and featuring a large statue of St. Ignatius of Loyola.
  • Visit the side chapel containing the silver casket with the sacred remains of St. Francis Xavier.
  • Explore the gallery of modern art within the basilica, showcasing religious themes.
  • Observe the intricate marble flooring and the fine carvings on the walls and pillars.
  • Attend a traditional mass for a deeply spiritual experience.
  • Walk through the adjacent garden and enjoy the peaceful, historic atmosphere of the Old Goa complex.

Location

The Basilica of Bom Jesus is located in Old Goa (Velha Goa), about 10 km east of Panaji.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The Feast of St. Francis Xavier (December 3rd) is the most important day of the year here.

How To Reach

By Air: Dabolim Airport is approx. 25 km away.

By Train: Karmali is the nearest railway station (approx. 2 km).

By Road: Very well-connected by road; frequent buses from Panaji and Madgaon bus terminals.

Travel Tips

- Maintain absolute silence and respect inside the basilica as it is a place of worship.
- Dress appropriately; avoid wearing overly revealing beachwear when visiting.
- Photography of the saint's remains is strictly prohibited.
- Visit during the early morning hours for a more tranquil experience before the tour buses arrive.
