Overview

Pawapuri, also known as Apapuri (the sinless city), is a supremely sacred place for Jains. It is the site where Lord Mahaveera, the 24th Tirthankara, attained Nirvana and was cremated in 527 BCE. The centerpiece of Pawapuri is the Jalmandir, an exquisite white marble temple situated in the middle of a large pond filled with blooming lotuses. According to legend, the pond was created by the massive excavation of earth by devotees who wanted to take a handful of holy soil from the site of the Lord's cremation.

Things To Do

  • Walk across the 600-foot-long stone bridge to reach the Jalmandir.
  • Offer prayers and meditate in the peaceful sanctum of the marble temple.
  • Visit the Samosharan Temple, built at the spot where Lord Mahaveera delivered his final sermon.
  • Admire the beauty of the thousands of lotuses that carpet the surrounding pond.
  • Visit the Gaon Mandir, the site where Lord Mahaveera breathed his last.
  • Participate in the special prayers during Diwali, which marks the anniversary of the Lord's Nirvana.

Location

Pawapuri is located in the Nalanda district, about 90 km from Patna and 38 km from Rajgir.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to March. The Diwali festival is the most significant and vibrant time to visit.

Top Attractions

Jalmandir Samosharan Temple Gaon Mandir Gunavataji (Nearby) Vishnu Pad Temple (Nearby)

How To Reach

By Air: Patna Airport (approx. 100 km) is the nearest major airport.

By Train: Pawapuri has a small station; Bihar Sharif (15 km) and Rajgir (38 km) are major nearby hubs.

By Road: Very well-connected by road on the main highway between Patna and Ranchi; regular buses available.

Travel Tips

- Wear modest clothing when visiting the temples.
- Remove footwear and leather items before entering the temple complex.
- Respect the Jain principles of non-violence (Ahimsa) while on the premises.
- Feeding the fish in the pond is a common practice; you can buy fish feed locally.
