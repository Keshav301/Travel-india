Overview

Munnar, a breathtakingly beautiful hill station in Kerala, is situated at the confluence of three mountain streams—Mudrappuza, Nallathanni, and Kundala. Once the summer resort of the British Government in South India, it's famous for its sprawling tea plantations, picture-book towns, winding lanes, and holiday facilities. Munnar is also home to Eravikulam National Park, where the endangered Nilgiri Tahr thrives, and Anamudi Peak, the highest peak in South India.

Things To Do

  • Take a guided tour through the emerald-green tea estates.
  • Visit the Tata Tea Museum to learn about tea processing history.
  • Explore Eravikulam National Park and spot the Nilgiri Tahr.
  • Enjoy boating at Mattupetty Dam and Kundala Lake.
  • Hike to Echo Point and hear your voice resonate across the hills.
  • Witness the Attukad Waterfalls in their full glory during or after monsoon.

Location

Munnar is located in the Idukki district of Kerala, in the Western Ghats mountain range.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from September to March for pleasant weather, and June to August for lush monsoon beauty.

Top Attractions

Eravikulam National Park Mattupetty Dam Tea Museum Anamudi Peak Echo Point

How To Reach

By Air: Cochin International Airport is the nearest (approx. 110 km).

By Train: Aluva or Ernakulam are the closest major railway stations.

By Road: Well-connected by scenic roads from Kochi, Madurai, and Coimbatore.

Travel Tips

- Carry a light sweater or jacket as it can get chilly in the evenings.
- Pre-book your entry to Eravikulam National Park online to avoid long queues.
- Wear comfortable walking shoes for exploring the tea gardens.
- Try the local "Munnar Tea" and various spices available at the markets.
