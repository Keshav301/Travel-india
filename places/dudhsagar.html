Overview

Dudhsagar Falls, literally translating to "Sea of Milk," is one of India's tallest waterfalls at 310 meters (1017 feet). Located on the Mandovi River on the Goa-Karnataka border, the waterfall cascades down the Western Ghats in four tiers. During the monsoon, the water flows with immense force, creating a foamy white appearance that gives the falls its name. The falls are situated within the Bhagwan Mahavir Wildlife Sanctuary and are a popular spot for adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts.

Things To Do

  • Take an adventurous 4x4 Jeep Safari through the Molem National Park to reach the falls.
  • Enjoy a refreshing swim in the plunge pool at the bottom of the waterfall (life jackets mandatory).
  • Trek through the lush Western Ghats for spectacular, up-close views (with authorized guides).
  • Witness the iconic sight of a train passing over the bridge right in front of the falls.
  • Go for a wildlife sighting tour in the Bhagwan Mahavir Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Visit the nearby ancient Mahadev Temple at Tambdi Surla, carved from black basalt.

Location

Dudhsagar Falls is located in Sanguem Taluka, in the south-eastern part of Goa, near the Karnataka border.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is from October to May. The falls are in full bloom during the monsoon (June-September), but the Jeep safari is often closed for safety.

How To Reach

By Air: Dabolim Airport (approx. 70 km away).

By Train: Kulem is the nearest railway station (approx. 8 km), from where Jeeps operate.

By Road: Reach Kulem by road; authorized Jeeps are the only allowed vehicles into the sanctuary.

Travel Tips

- Book your Jeep safari tickets online in advance to avoid long wait times.
- Carry change of clothes as you might get wet near the falls or by swimming.
- Keep the environment clean; do not carry plastic or feed the monkeys.
- Wear sturdy footwear with good grip for walking on rocky areas.
